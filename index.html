Education + Plans



Faith Christian School

I'm a senior at FCS, where I've gone since kindergarten. Going to a small Christian school, I've been involved in volleyball, track, cheerleading, chapel committee, Senior Council, orchestra, and Honor Society. At FCS I found my passions, my faith, and my friends.

Favorite Travels



Tirana, Albania

The summer after my freshman year, I went on my first mission trip. My youth group was going to Tirana, Albania, where we met up with a local church to offer our help and support. After a week spending time with the youth group, helping out with various projects, and sightseeing, we headed into the mountains to run a week-long youth camp. We were all camp counselors, and I was with the middle-school aged girls. We made lots of close friends and got to witness lives changed by the Gospel. After that trip, I knew I wanted to spend more of my time and effort sharing the Good News of Christ, not just around the world, but even in my own commmunity. The next summer, I went back to Albania and saw God prove His faithfulness to us yet again and in countless ways.



Mexico City

The winter in between my two trips to Albania, I went to Mexico City with a group from school. We stayed with missionaries in the city and ran sports camps at the local parks and elementary schools. While I experienced a greater language barrier than I did in Albania, we bonded with many of the kids and were able to encourage their walk with Christ. My favorite memory was playing Four Square in the church courtyard with the students from youth group; even though we couldn't understand each other, we all had a blast.



Washington DC

My junior year, I participated in an essay competition to be a part of a trip to Washington DC during senior year. Myself, eight other students, and two teachers packed up in the van to head East. We experienced everything from Pennsylvania scrapple, to exclusive tours of the Eisenhower building and the Cabinet offices. In a way, this trip symbolized to me both the adventures that come with senior year, and the world I can look forward to being a part of post-graduation.
